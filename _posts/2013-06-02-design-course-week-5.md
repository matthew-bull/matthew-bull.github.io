These are some thoughs about week 5 from my coursera design course - [Design: Creation of Artifacts in Society](https://www.coursera.org/course/design).

This has been a quiet week because of the national holidays in the UK and US. However there were still some videos to watch, things to read, and design concepts to think about.

In particular the course focussed on Karl T. Ulrich's idea of the design process being one of competition. You start with a lot of alternative, and gradually whittle these down to a single, final product.

Personally I feel the word 'competition' is a little loaded, although I can see that in the real world of design a competition is exactly how it ends up. For example, architects will vye for their design to be chosen through a competition process where designs are voted out by some kind of committee.

At a more abstract level you can maybe think of this process as a form of *evolution*. There are certain parameters to success, although those parameters are constantly changing. Early options may get eliminated *at a point in time* because they didn't meet the parameters as well as other options. This doesn't mean they're necessarily inferior: it's just that at that moment they were less suitable.

Revisiting options at a later date may give us other alternatives. This is often what we might call fashion.
